  ### **Scenario Title: The Philadelphia Compromise**

  **Learning Objectives:**
  Upon completion of this scenario, the student will be able to:

  1.  [cite\_start]Analyze the competing interests of large and small states regarding legislative representation during the 
  Constitutional Convention[cite: 261].
  2.  [cite\_start]Evaluate the political and ethical trade-offs required to address the issue of slavery in the Constitution[cite:
   337, 347].
  3.  [cite\_start]Synthesize different viewpoints to understand why pragmatic compromise was essential to the creation of the U.S.
   Constitution[cite: 25].

  **Learner Role:** You are a delegate from **Connecticut**, a state with a middling population, caught between the powerful large 
  states and the numerous small states. [cite\_start]Your colleague, Roger Sherman, is looking to you for support as you both 
  navigate the contentious debates[cite: 323].

  **Tracking Variables:**
  This scenario will track two key variables to determine the outcome:

    * **`LargeStateFavor`:** A score from -5 to +5. Positive scores indicate favor with large states like Virginia; negative scores
   indicate favor with small states like New Jersey.
    * **`SouthernStateFavor`:** A score from -5 to +5. Positive scores indicate favor with southern, slaveholding states; negative 
  scores indicate favor with northern states.
